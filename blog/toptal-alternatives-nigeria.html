Toptal Alternatives for Nigeria: 5 Platforms Where You Actually Earn More

July 13, 2026 · 9 min read · By Freelance LeadsHub

Toptal is good. But for Nigerian freelancers, it's not the only option—and honestly, it might not be the best one for your wallet.

The reality: Toptal takes 20% commission on your first $500 earned, then 10% after that. For a developer making $50/hour, that's real money gone every month. Factor in Toptal's vetting process (which takes weeks), strict project requirements, and the fact that they're still competing you against thousands of others, and you start to wonder if there's a better path.

There is. This guide covers Toptal alternatives for Nigeria that actually work—plus a strategy that's making smarter Nigerian freelancers 30–50% more per month.

Why Toptal Isn't the Answer for Most Nigerian Freelancers

Before we get into alternatives, let's be real about Toptal's limitations:

  • High vetting barrier: Toptal's screening process rejects 97% of applicants. If you're just starting out, you likely won't get in.
  • Commission eats profit: 10–20% comes straight out of your pocket. On a ₦500,000 monthly project, you're giving up ₦50,000–100,000.
  • Payment delays: Toptal pays weekly, but transfers to Nigerian bank accounts or Wise can take 2–5 business days. At current exchange rates (~₦1,550 per USD), delays cost you real money if the rate moves against you.
  • Limited project scope: Toptal focuses on longer-term contracts. If you want quick one-off projects, you're better served elsewhere.
  • You're still competing: Being on Toptal doesn't mean you get consistent work. You still have to pitch for projects and compete with other "top" freelancers.

The smarter play? Mix platforms strategically—and better yet, skip the middleman entirely.

5 Toptal Alternatives Actually Used by Nigerian Freelancers

1. Gun.io (Lower Commissions, Better Payment Terms)

Gun.io is Toptal's quieter cousin. They vet freelancers (less strict than Toptal), take only 15% commission, and focus on long-term software development work.

  • Commission: 15% flat (no tiered reduction)
  • Payment: Bi-weekly via Wise or PayPal
  • Vetting difficulty: Moderate (easier than Toptal)
  • Typical rate: $40–80/hour for mid-level developers in Nigeria

One Gun.io developer from Lagos told us he makes $4,000–5,500/month on the platform. At current rates, that's ₦6.2–8.5 million monthly. Not bad—but he's spending 5+ hours per week on pitches.

2. Upwork Pro (If You're Willing to Do the Work)

Yes, Upwork. But hear us out: the *Pro* tier (₦750/month) connects you to higher-budget clients and gives you better visibility. Standard Upwork takes 20% on the first $500, then 10% after. Pro takes 10% across the board after you hit $500 in earnings.

  • Commission: 10% (if you reach $500 earnings)
  • Payment: Weekly to Wise/bank account
  • Client quality: Varies wildly, but dedicated account managers exist for top earners
  • Realistic income: ₦300,000–800,000/month (depends entirely on your pitch rate and niche)

Nigerian context: Upwork data shows Nigerian developers earn 15–30% less than equivalents in India or the Philippines. Why? Perception. To beat this, you need strong portfolio pieces and reviews. Takes 3–6 months to build momentum.

3. Topmate / Maven (Direct Client Relationships)

These platforms let you set your own price, take 10–15% commission, and build recurring relationships. No bidding wars.

  • Commission: 10–15%
  • Payment: Wise, PayPal, direct bank transfer
  • Work type: Consulting, tutoring, code reviews, training
  • Income potential: ₦1–3 million/month (if you market yourself)

The catch: these platforms work best if you already have an audience or strong personal brand. A UI designer with 5,000 Twitter followers using Topmate can charge ₦50,000–150,000 per consultation and book 5–10 sessions monthly. ₦250k–1.5M/month from 10 hours of work.

4. Remoteok.io (Passive Job Board with No Commission)

Post your profile once. Jobs come to you. No middleman, no commission, no bidding.

  • Commission: 0% (you negotiate payment directly)
  • Payment: Direct with the client
  • Vetting: Light (you post your profile, clients contact you)
  • Realistic income: ₦400k–2M/month (depends on your niche and visibility)

The downside: you have to handle contracts, invoicing, and chasing payments yourself. But for experienced freelancers, this is huge. A data analyst on Remoteok making $60/hour sees ₦4.5M monthly (at ₦1,550/$). With 0% commission, that's 100% yours.

5. PeoplePerHour (Underrated, Easier Vetting)

UK-based platform, less saturated with Nigerian freelancers than Upwork, takes 20% commission but has a large pool of steady client work.

  • Commission: 20% for first 2 projects, then 15%
  • Payment: Weekly via Wise or PayPal
  • Vetting: Easy (portfolio review, no screening exam)
  • Realistic income: ₦250k–700k/month

Why use it? Lower competition from your local market. A web designer with 3–4 live projects on PeoplePerHour earns steadily while building the portfolio to move to Remoteok or direct clients.

The Real Talk: None of these platforms will make you rich. Even at ₦2M/month, you're trading hours for money. The freelancers making ₦5M+ monthly are either (1) running agencies with other people, (2) selling digital products, or (3) working directly with 5–10 retainer clients at ₦800k–1.5M each. Platforms are stepping stones, not destinations.

Handling Nigerian Bank & Payment Issues

One thing Toptal does handle: USD payments without stress. If you're switching platforms, here's the real setup:

  • Wise Account: Open one (free, takes 20 minutes). All platforms pay to Wise first. Transfer to your Nigerian account same day at true exchange rates (beats CBN rates by ₦50–100 per dollar).
  • Payoneer (legacy): Still works but charges higher fees. Avoid if Wise is available.
  • Nigerian Bank Accounts: Most platforms can send directly to Zenith, GTBank, or Access Bank. But Wise is faster and cheaper.
  • Exchange Rate Strategy: Don't transfer immediately after earning. Keep USD in Wise for 3–7 days to catch favorable rates. During economic stress, rates swing ₦50+ per dollar. Timing saves ₦250k+ monthly on big transfers.

Real timeline: Monday you finish a project on Upwork ($500). Tuesday it settles. Wednesday it's in Wise. Thursday morning you convert to NGN at ₦1,550/$. You have ₦775,000 in your Nigerian bank account by Thursday EOD.

This beats Toptal's weekly payout, which sometimes takes 8–10 business days to hit your account.
