Payoneer vs Grey vs Wise for Nigerian Freelancers 2025: Which Payment Method Wins?

18 July 2026 · 9 min read · By Freelance LeadsHub

You landed a $500 project on Upwork. Your client approved the contract. Work is done. Now comes the real question: how do you actually get that money into your Nigerian bank account without losing 15–20% to fees?

If you're a Nigerian freelancer working with international clients, Payoneer vs Grey vs Wise for Nigerian freelancers is THE decision that affects your monthly take-home. At the current naira exchange rate (around 1,500–1,650 NGN per USD), every percentage point of fees you avoid means real money back in your account.

This article compares all three platforms head-to-head with real numbers so you can stop guessing and start choosing based on math.

Platform Breakdown: Payoneer vs Grey vs Wise

Payoneer

Setup: Payoneer is the veteran. It's been in Nigeria for over a decade. Nearly every Nigerian freelancer has heard of it or used it.

Fees (2025):

  • Receiving money from clients: Free (for most platforms like Upwork, Fiverr)
  • Withdrawal to Nigerian bank: ₦550–₦800 per transaction (flat fee)
  • Exchange rate markup: 1.5–2.5% worse than real-time rate
  • Card issuance (Payoneer Mastercard): Free
  • ATM withdrawal: $1.95 USD per withdrawal

Real example: You withdraw $500 USD to your First Bank account.

  • Real-time rate: 1 USD = 1,550 NGN
  • Payoneer's rate: 1 USD = 1,512 NGN (2.5% markup)
  • You receive: ₦756,000
  • Bank fee (if charged): −₦650
  • Net: ₦755,350
  • Effective rate: 1 USD = 1,510.7 NGN (2.5% loss)

Timeline: Withdrawal typically hits your account in 1–3 business days. Reliable, but not the fastest.

Best for: Beginners who want simplicity and don't mind paying a bit more for ease of use. If you're earning under $1,000/month, the flat fees hurt less.

Grey (formerly known as Grey Money)

Setup: Grey is newer, built specifically for the African creator and freelancer economy. It's positioned as the "fintech for African creatives."

Fees (2025):

  • Receiving money: Free
  • Withdrawal to Nigerian bank: 1% of amount + ₦100 per transaction
  • Exchange rate markup: 0.5–1% (the best in this comparison)
  • Instant transfers available: Yes (for a premium, see below)
  • Card: Available but charges vary

Real example: Same $500 USD withdrawal.

  • Real-time rate: 1 USD = 1,550 NGN
  • Grey's rate: 1 USD = 1,543 NGN (0.5% markup)
  • Conversion: $500 × 1,543 = ₦771,500
  • Grey fee: 1% of ₦771,500 = ₦7,715 + ₦100 = ₦7,815
  • Bank fee: ₦0–₦300 (varies by bank; some don't charge)
  • Net: ~₦763,385
  • Effective rate: 1 USD = 1,526.8 NGN (1.5% loss)

Timeline: Standard transfers: 1–2 business days. Instant transfers available but come with additional fees (usually ₦500–₦2,000 for priority).

Best for: Mid-tier freelancers ($1,000–$5,000/month) who care about exchange rates and want modern fintech infrastructure. The lower markup is a real advantage.

Wise (formerly TransferWise)

Setup: Wise is the global gold standard for international transfers. It uses real-time mid-market rates and has the lowest fees in the industry.

Fees (2025):

  • Receiving money: Depends on source platform. Upwork/Fiverr integration: Usually free
  • Withdrawal to Nigerian bank: 1% of amount (no flat fee) + potential bank charges
  • Exchange rate markup: 0 (uses actual mid-market rate)
  • Wise account: Free to open
  • Card: Available (Wise debit card) with low fees

Real example: Same $500 USD withdrawal.

  • Real-time mid-market rate: 1 USD = 1,550 NGN
  • Wise's rate: 1 USD = 1,550 NGN (no markup)
  • Conversion: $500 × 1,550 = ₦775,000
  • Wise fee: 1% of ₦775,000 = ₦7,750
  • Bank fee: ₦0–₦300
  • Net: ~₦767,200
  • Effective rate: 1 USD = 1,534.4 NGN (1% loss)

Timeline: 1–3 business days depending on your Nigerian bank. Can be slower than Grey in practice because Wise has fewer Nigerian bank partnerships.

Best for: High-volume earners ($3,000+/month) where the 1% fee on large amounts saves serious money. Also the best for sending money internationally if you ever need to.

Key insight: On a $500 withdrawal, the difference between all three is roughly ₦8,000–₦12,000 (about 1.5–2%). On a $5,000 withdrawal, that's ₦150,000–₦200,000 difference per month. At $10,000/month, you're talking ₦300,000+ annually in fees you could avoid by choosing Wise or Grey over Payoneer.

Difference: Wise puts an extra ₦820,200 per year in your pocket compared to Payoneer. That's 3 months of rent in most Nigerian cities. At $5,000/month, you're looking at ₦1.6+ million extra per year with Wise.

How to Set Up Each Platform

Payoneer

  1. Go to payoneer.com
  2. Sign up with your email and phone number
  3. Verify your identity (they'll ask for ID)
  4. Connect to Upwork, Fiverr, or ask clients to pay directly
  5. Add your Nigerian bank account for withdrawals

Grey

  1. Download the Grey app or visit grey.money
  2. Sign up with email or phone number
  3. Complete KYC verification (takes 10–15 minutes)
  4. Connect your bank account
  5. Request your Grey USD account details to share with clients

Wise

  1. Go to wise.com
  2. Sign up with email
  3. Verify your identity
  4. Add your Nigerian bank account
  5. Get your Wise USD account details (you can share this with clients for direct payments)

Which Platform Should You Choose?

If you're earning under $1,000/month: Start with Payoneer. It's simple, everyone knows it, and the absolute fee difference is small. Get your systems right first.

If you're earning $1,000–$5,000/month: Switch to Grey. The 1% fee + better exchange rate saves you ₦30,000–₦50,000+ per month. That's real money, and Grey's interface is modern.

If you're earning over $5,000/month: Use Wise. The 1% flat fee on large amounts is unbeatable. Plus, if you ever need to send money internationally (to buy inventory, pay contractors, etc.), Wise is the cheapest option by far.

Pro move: Use more than one. Keep Payoneer for Upwork (it's integrated). Use Grey for direct clients. Switch to Wise once you hit $5,000/month. Spread your risk and optimize each transaction.

Other Factors to Consider

Customer Support

  • Payoneer: 24/7 chat support, but responses are slow (12–24 hours)
  • Grey: In-app support, fast for simple issues, slower for complex ones
  • Wise: Email and in-app support, reliable but not as fast as Payoneer's volume

Safety & Reliability

All three are legitimate and regulated. Wise is the most established globally (founded 2011). Payoneer has been serving Africa for 15+ years. Grey is newer but backed by solid investors. You won't lose money with any of them.

Multiple Withdrawals Per Month

If you withdraw weekly or bi-weekly, Grey's flat 1% beats Payoneer's flat ₦550+ per transaction. Wise stays competitive at 1%.
