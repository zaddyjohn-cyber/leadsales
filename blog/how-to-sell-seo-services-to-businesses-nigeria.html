How to Sell SEO Services to Local Businesses in Nigeria: A Practical Guide for Freelancers

2026-07-20 · 9 min read · By Freelance LeadsHub

If you're a freelancer in Nigeria with SEO skills, you've probably spent months grinding on Upwork and Fiverr, competing on price, dealing with platform fees that eat 20% of your earnings, and waiting days for proposals to convert. There's a better way.

This guide shows you exactly how to sell SEO services to local businesses in Nigeria by bypassing the middleman platforms entirely and going direct to decision-makers. You'll learn realistic pricing in both Naira and USD, how to find the right businesses to pitch, what to say in your first email, and how much you can actually earn doing this.

Why Local Nigerian Businesses Need SEO (And Why They'll Pay You)

Most local Nigerian businesses—e-commerce stores, real estate agents, law firms, logistics companies, consultants—are invisible on Google. They either don't have websites, or their websites rank on page 15 for searches their customers are making every day.

Here's the gap they need filled:

  • Visibility: A pharmacy in Lagos doesn't show up when someone searches "best pharmacy near Ikoyi." They lose customers daily.
  • No system: They don't understand Google ranking, keywords, or how SEO works. They just know competitors get more calls than they do.
  • Budget reality: They can't afford the ₦500,000+ that Lagos digital agencies charge. They want someone reliable at ₦50,000–₦150,000/month.
  • Local search dominance: Once you get them on Google Maps and local search results, they see ROI quickly—more calls, more foot traffic, more online orders.

This is your opening. You're offering affordable, results-driven SEO to businesses that have money but no SEO expert.

Realistic Pricing for SEO Services in Nigeria

Let's talk numbers honestly. Your pricing depends on:

  • Niche: E-commerce and real estate pay more. Local services (plumbers, tailors) pay less.
  • Competition level: Ranking in Ikeja is harder than ranking in a smaller town. Price accordingly.
  • Your experience: A beginner charges ₦30,000–₦60,000/month. An experienced freelancer charges ₦100,000–₦300,000/month.

Pricing Breakdown (Monthly Retainer Model)

Starter Package: ₦40,000–₦60,000/month (~$25–$40 USD)

  • Keyword research for 1–2 high-intent keywords
  • On-page optimization (title tags, meta descriptions, headers)
  • 10–15 high-quality backlinks per month
  • Monthly rank tracking report
  • Best for: Small businesses, local services, startups

Standard Package: ₦80,000–₦150,000/month (~$50–$100 USD)

  • Keyword research (5–10 keywords)
  • Technical SEO audit and fixes
  • Content creation (2–3 optimized blog posts)
  • 25–30 backlinks/month
  • Google My Business optimization
  • Best for: E-commerce stores, real estate, professional services

Premium Package: ₦200,000–₦500,000+/month (~$130–$330+ USD)

  • Full SEO strategy and competitor analysis
  • Content calendar (4–6 posts/month)
  • Advanced link building with outreach
  • Technical SEO + site speed optimization
  • Weekly check-ins and strategy calls
  • Best for: Larger e-commerce, agencies, established brands

Most Nigerian businesses you'll pitch to will want the Starter or Standard package. That's realistic.

Real Income Projection: If you land 10 clients at ₦100,000/month each, that's ₦1,000,000/month (~$650 USD). It usually takes 3–4 months to build a roster of 10 clients from cold outreach. That's a sustainable ₦300,000–₦650,000 in your first quarter working directly with businesses.

Cold Email Strategy: What Actually Works

Once you have a lead list, the next step is cold email. But not spam—strategic, personalized outreach.

Step 1: Segment Your List

Don't send the same email to a real estate agent and a plumber. Split your leads by niche:

  • E-commerce/online stores
  • Real estate/property
  • Professional services (law, consulting, accounting)
  • Local services (salons, gyms, clinics)
  • Restaurants/hospitality

Write one email template per segment.

Step 2: Write a Short, Benefit-Focused Email

Here's a template that works in Nigeria:

Subject: [Business Name] + Google visibility

Hi [Owner Name],

I checked your website—you're missing out on customers searching for [their service] on Google right now.

I helped [similar business] in [their city] get 45 more calls/month by ranking them for [keyword]. Same thing is possible for you.

Takes 2 weeks to see movement. Costs ₦50k–₦150k/month depending on your niche.

Free 15-min call if you're curious?

—[Your name]

That's it. No fluff. You're telling them:

  1. Their problem (invisible on Google)
  2. Your proof (client case study)
  3. Timeline and cost (no surprises)
  4. Clear ask (a call)

Expect a 2–5% response rate from a quality lead list. That means 200 leads = 4–10 calls scheduled.

Step 4: The Call Script

When they reply, keep the call short (10–15 minutes). Your goal: understand their pain and give a no-pressure quote.

Ask:

  • "How many calls/customers are you getting per month right now?"
  • "Do people find you on Google when they search for [your service]?"
  • "What's your biggest challenge with online visibility?"

Listen. Then say:

"Based on what you've told me, I'd recommend [Starter/Standard] package. That's [price in Naira]. I'll give you a rank report every month so you see the movement. Sound fair?"

If they say yes, send a contract. If they're unsure, offer a 2-week trial at 50% off.

How to Deliver SEO Results They'll Actually Notice

This is critical. You need to deliver or they won't renew, and you won't get referrals.

Month 1: Foundation

  • Keyword research (target keywords they should rank for)
  • On-page optimization (titles, descriptions, headers, internal links)
  • Google My Business setup/optimization (critical for local search)
  • First 10 backlinks from relevant sites

Month 2-3: Momentum

  • Blog posts (2–3/month, optimized for their keywords)
  • Link building campaign (20–30 links/month)
  • Site speed optimization (impacts rankings)
  • First keyword rankings (many will move into top 30–50)

Month 4+: Results

  • Keywords ranking in top 10 (more visibility)
  • Increased organic traffic (trackable in Google Analytics)
  • More calls/form submissions from Google
  • Client sees ROI, renews contract

Track everything in a monthly report. Show them rankings, traffic, and calls/leads from Google. Make it visual. Nigerian business owners respond to data.

Managing Multiple Clients Without Burning Out

The scalability question: Can you handle 15 SEO clients at once?

Yes, but strategize:

  • Use templates: Create standard on-page optimization checklists, blog post outlines, and link-building workflows you can reuse.
  • Batch work: Do all keyword research on Mondays, all content creation on Tuesdays, all link outreach on Wednesdays.
  • Automation: Use tools like SE Ranking or Ahrefs for rank tracking (automatic monthly reports), and cold email templates to streamline outreach.
  • Hire help: Once you hit 12+ clients, hire a virtual assistant (₦30,000/month) to handle admin and basic content tasks.

Realistically, one person can manage 15–20 SEO clients without subcontracting, generating ₦1.5M–₦2M/month in revenue.

Converting More Leads: Booking More Sales Calls

If your response rate is low, you might be:

  • Emailing the wrong person (pitch the owner or marketing manager, not the receptionist)
  • Sending generic emails (personalize. Mention their business name, a competitor, something specific)
  • Asking for too much too soon (just ask for a 15-min call, not their business)
  • Using poor email timing (send at 9am or 2pm, not 6pm or midnight)
