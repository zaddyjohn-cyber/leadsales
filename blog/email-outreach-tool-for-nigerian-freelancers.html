Common Mistakes Nigerian Freelancers Make With Cold Email

I want to save you time and frustration. Here are the mistakes that kill campaigns:

Mistake 1: Sending Generic Emails

Wrong: "Hi, I'm a freelancer offering web design services..."

Right: "Hi Adekunle, I noticed your store [Store Name] is getting traffic but checkout is slow. I fixed the same issue for [Similar Business]—30% faster checkout = 24% more revenue."

Show you've researched them. Show results. Make it personal.

Mistake 2: Sending Too Many Emails Too Fast

If you dump 500 emails in one day, email providers flag you as spam. Your domain reputation tanks. Use Zaram Web Mailer to space emails out over days (10–20 per day maximum).

Mistake 3: Not Following Up

73% of cold email conversions happen on follow-up #3, #4, or #5. One email is not enough. Your tool should automate this: if no reply, send a follow-up 3 days later, then 5 days later, then 7 days later.

Mistake 4: Using an Unprofessional Email Address

Don't send cold email from a Gmail or Yahoo account. Use your own domain (yourdomain.com). It takes 10 minutes to set up with Namecheap or GoDaddy, and it looks professional. Big difference in reply rates.

Mistake 5: Not Tracking Anything

If you can't see who opened your email, who clicked, and who replied, you can't improve. Zaram Web Mailer tracks all of this automatically. Use the data to refine your subject lines, your message, and your target audience.

Pro tip: The best cold email campaigns aren't built in a day. Test 50 emails, analyze results, tweak your message, send another 50. By the third round, your reply rate usually doubles.
