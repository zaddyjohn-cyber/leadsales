Cold Email vs Upwork Proposals: Which Works Better in 2025 for Nigerian Freelancers?

2026-07-22 · 9 min read · By Freelance LeadsHub

You're a Nigerian freelancer. You've got skills—web design, copywriting, virtual assistance, whatever. But here's the real question: where do you actually get paying clients?

For years, the answer has been Upwork. Post a profile, bid on jobs, hope. But by 2025, something has shifted. More and more Nigerian freelancers are ditching the platform wars and going directly to businesses via cold email. The question isn't "should I use Upwork?" anymore. The real question is: cold email vs Upwork proposals—which actually works better?

I'm going to give you the honest answer based on real numbers, real timelines, and real Nigerian context. Not theory. Not hype. Just what works.

The Upwork Model: What You Already Know (and What's Changed)

You know Upwork. You've probably already spent hours there.

Here's how it works: you create a profile, bid on jobs, and if you win, you work through the Upwork platform. Upwork takes 5–20% of your earnings depending on your JSS (Job Success Score) and client history.

The Real Numbers on Upwork (2025)

  • Time to first client: 2–4 weeks if you're new. Often longer.
  • Win rate on proposals: 3–8% for new freelancers. You bid on 100 jobs, you might win 3–8.
  • Average hourly rate: $8–15/hour for Nigerian freelancers in 2025 (₦13,000–₦24,000 at ₦1,600:$1 exchange rate). Senior freelancers with strong profiles can hit $20–30/hour.
  • Upwork's cut: 5% (if you've earned $500+), but also time spent on bidding—often 5–15 hours per week finding and writing proposals with low conversion.
  • Payment timeline: 14 days after project completion. Then conversion via Payoneer/Wise to NGN. Total time to naira: 2–3 weeks.
  • Client quality: Mixed. Budget-conscious clients, lots of scope creep, revisions without end.

Reality check: A Nigerian freelancer earning $15/hour on Upwork, working 30 hours per week, brings in $450/week. That's roughly ₦720,000/month. Sounds good. But subtract:

  • Upwork's 5% fee: ₦36,000
  • Payoneer withdrawal fee: ~₦6,000
  • Internet/data costs (NEPA is unreliable, so data is essential): ₦8,000–10,000/month
  • Taxes (freelancers should set aside 10–15%): ~₦80,000

Take-home: roughly ₦600,000/month. Not bad. But here's the catch: that ₦720,000 assumes you're consistently winning bids and working 30 hours. Most new freelancers? They're spending 40–50 hours per week to land those 30 working hours.

Cold Email: The Alternative Path

Cold email works differently. You don't bid. You don't compete. You find a business that needs what you offer, and you send them a direct message.

How Cold Email Actually Works

  1. Get a targeted list of businesses: Find companies in your niche (e.g., web design, content writing, social media management) in a specific country or region. This is where tools like Freelance LeadsHub come in—you can download business leads (name, email, phone, website) sorted by niche and country in minutes instead of weeks.
  2. Write one good email: Not 100 variations. One email template that explains what you do and why they need it.
  3. Send to your list: Use a bulk email tool like Zaram Web Mailer to send your email to 100, 500, or 1,000 businesses at once. The tool handles follow-ups and helps you avoid spam filters.
  4. Wait for replies: Typical response rate? 1–5% from cold email. That means 100 emails = 1–5 conversations. 500 emails = 5–25 conversations.
  5. Close deals: Those conversations become clients. Usually at 3–5x the rate you'd get on Upwork.

The key difference: you're not competing on price. You're selling directly to the business.

Real Numbers: Cold Email for Nigerian Freelancers

  • Time to first client: 1–2 weeks. Some sooner if your list is warm and your email is good.
  • Conversion rate: 1–3% of emails sent typically result in a paid project. That's much lower volume but much higher quality.
  • Average project value: $300–1,000+ per project (depending on your niche). A web design client? Could be $500–2,000. Content writing? $200–500 per article, but often recurring.
  • Hourly equivalent: Let's say you land a $500 web design project that takes 20 hours. That's $25/hour. Before fees.
  • Fees: Much lower than Upwork. Direct bank transfer via Wise or Payoneer (same 1–2% fee). No platform cut unless you use a payment processor.
  • Payment timeline: Negotiable. Often 50% upfront, 50% on completion. Faster cash flow than Upwork's 14-day hold.
  • Client quality: High. Businesses who reach out to freelancers directly are often serious, willing to pay, and more respectful of scope.

Real Example: A Nigerian copywriter sends 200 cold emails to UK e-commerce businesses. Gets 6 replies (3% response rate). Closes 2 clients at $400/month retainer each = $800/month recurring revenue. That's ₦1.28 million/month with zero competition and no bidding. Compare that to spending 40 hours/week on Upwork proposals for sporadic $50 jobs.

Why Cold Email Works Better (Even for New Freelancers)

1. You're Not Competing on Price

On Upwork, 50 freelancers are bidding for the same job. The client picks the cheapest or the one with the best reviews. You're in a race to the bottom.

With cold email, you're the only one pitching. The business didn't post a job; they're not comparing you to 49 others. You're offering a solution to a problem they probably didn't know they could solve. That's a different conversation entirely.

2. You Build a Real Business, Not a Gig

Upwork is a platform. You're one of millions. Your profile can be buried, algorithms change, the platform can restrict your account, or they can raise their fees tomorrow.

Cold email builds direct relationships. Those clients become your clients, not Upwork's. If you land 5 cold email clients at $500/month each, that's recurring revenue that's not dependent on any platform staying stable.

3. You Can Scale Without Adding Hours

On Upwork, to earn more, you either increase rates (risky) or work more hours (unsustainable). With cold email, you can send 500 emails in the same time it takes to send 50 Upwork proposals. Your effort stays roughly the same; your reach multiplies.

4. Better Client Fit

When you cold email a web design agency, a local online store, or a digital marketing firm, you know they're the type who uses freelancers. They're not first-time buyers; they're not looking for the cheapest option. They want someone who understands their niche.

On Upwork, you get clients who posted a job because they wanted to see what they could get for cheap.

Common Objections (and Why They Don't Hold Up)

"Cold email is spam. Businesses hate it."

No. Spam is irrelevant offers to wrong people. A targeted email about your service to a business that actually needs it? That's outreach. And it works—companies like Mailchimp, HubSpot, and Calendly were built partly on cold email. It's a legitimate business tool.

"I don't have a list of emails."

That's why Freelance LeadsHub exists. A few naira gets you hundreds of verified emails. Cheaper and faster than Upwork's bidding fees.

"What if no one replies?"

Then you didn't test your message, your list, or your niche. But statistically: 100 cold emails to the right businesses in your niche = 1–5 conversations. Most freelancers never test. That's why they assume it doesn't work.

"Upwork is guaranteed work. Cold email is uncertain."

So is Upwork. Your account can be suspended. Your profile can be buried. Clients can give you bad reviews. At least with cold email, if one client is difficult, you have other clients. You're not dependent on one platform's algorithm.

The Hybrid Approach (Honestly, This Is Best)

Here's what actually works best in 2025: use both.

Keep an Upwork profile. It's free, and some good clients find you there. But don't spend 40 hours per week bidding. Spend 5–10 hours.

Spend the majority of your time on cold email. Land 2–3 solid cold email clients at $500–1,000 each. That's your stable, recurring revenue. Then use Upwork for overflow work or quick projects.

Result: You're not dependent on any one platform. Your rates are higher. Your clients are better. Your stress is lower.

If you're serious about this, start with how to get clients as a freelancer in Nigeria for a broader strategy, then dive into cold email specifically.
